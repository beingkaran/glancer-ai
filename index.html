What is AIOps and Observability?

Observability is the practice of understanding the internal state of a software system from the data it emits — primarily metrics, logs and traces. As applications moved from monoliths to distributed microservices running on containers and serverless platforms, traditional monitoring — which only answers questions you knew to ask in advance — stopped being enough. Observability lets engineers ask new questions about unexpected behavior without shipping new code.

AIOps (Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations) builds on top of observability data. It applies machine learning and analytics to the enormous volume of events, metrics, logs and traces that modern systems produce, automating tasks such as anomaly detection, event correlation, root cause analysis and even automated remediation. The goal is to reduce noise, shorten the time to detect and resolve incidents, and let teams operate at a scale humans cannot manage manually.

Application Performance Monitoring (APM) sits at the intersection of the two: it focuses specifically on the performance, availability and user experience of applications — tracing transactions across services, surfacing slow code, and tying technical metrics to business outcomes.
