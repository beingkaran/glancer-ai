About Glancer.ai

A free, independent reference for the language of modern monitoring.

Our mission

Observability, AIOps and Application Performance Monitoring have become essential to running reliable software — but the vocabulary is sprawling, overlapping, and often defined differently by every vendor and blog post. Glancer.ai exists to fix that. We bring the terminology of these disciplines into one clean, searchable place so that engineers, students, product managers and curious readers can find a clear definition in seconds.

Who it's for

  • Engineers and SREs who want a quick, reliable reference while building dashboards, writing alerts or debugging incidents.
  • Newcomers and students learning observability for the first time and trying to make sense of the jargon.
  • DevOps, platform and product teams who need a shared vocabulary across roles.
  • Anyone evaluating monitoring tools who keeps running into unfamiliar terms.

How our definitions are researched

Each entry is written in plain English and aims to describe how a term is actually used across the industry. We study how leading observability and APM platforms — including Datadog, Dynatrace, New Relic, Splunk, AppDynamics and Broadcom — describe these concepts in their public documentation, and then distill that into a vendor-neutral explanation. Where a term originates from or is strongly associated with a specific platform (for example, Dynatrace's PurePath or AppDynamics' Business Transaction), we note that context.

Independence note: Glancer.ai is an independent educational project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any monitoring vendor. Product and company names are trademarks of their respective owners and are referenced only for identification and education.

How the search works

The search box on the home page uses a ranked, typo-tolerant matching engine that runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent anywhere. It checks exact matches, abbreviations, word boundaries, related terms and even definition text, and offers "did you mean" suggestions when you mistype. Everything is instant because the entire glossary loads with the page.

Help us improve

Spotted a term we're missing, or a definition you'd refine? We'd genuinely like to hear from you. Please reach out via our contact page — community feedback is how this glossary grows and stays accurate.
